Journal Entry #13: Testing Solar Charger

Does my solar powered charger work? October 21, 2018

Once creating the first draft of my product, I went back and re-evaluated it. I frequently tested my product and doing so led me to some important conclusions. First and foremost, if there is not enough sunlight or light energy (<5V) or more than enough light or sunlight (>5V), then the phone will not properly charge. Secondly, while initially assuming my product to be 100% successful, there are instances where charging is less efficient than it should be. This, as a result, led me to the idea of adding in a second solar panel, which I may or may not implement. I will make that decision in the coming days. In conclusion however, I have learned that the amount of light must produce around 5V. No more or no less voltage will work. However, if not enough sunlight is available, through trial and error, I determined that a 16W LED light projecting 100W equivalent of light directly put upon the solar panels with only 10cm of distance apart will be able to successfully charge a phone.
