Journal Entry #14: Product Evaluation - Improving Product Efficiency with Added Solar Panel

Adding another solar panel, October 24, 2018

I have decided to move forward with the idea of adding a second solar panel. The purpose of the solar panel is to determine if the efficiency and charging time of my solar powered phone charger will increase. Through trial last time, I determined that a one solar panelled phone charger would charge a phone by 1 percent every 20 to 25 minutes (ignoring inconsistencies with charging). The results after five trials with one solar panel will be illustrated below. After soldering on the second solar panel today, I tried to test charging time once again, and results did show slight improvement, but inconsistently once again. When charging properly, the solar powered charger, now with two solar panels, would charge the phone on average in 10-15 minutes. However, when I say inconsistencies, I mean the phone would after a while not charge as effectively, or even lose charge. While I determined how to improve charging time through added solar power, I have yet to solve the inconsistent charging issues with my product. However, I have a hypothesis that I may test out if time allows. A diode is an electrical component that prevents current from moving back. Adding this component to my charger may help the current in my charge move in one forward direction that could provide consistent charge to a phone.
