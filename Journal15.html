Journal Entry #15: Assessing Environmental Impacts of my Device

Carbon Offset from Solar Powered Phone Charger, October 25, 2018

After completing some research about solar energy and its environmental impacts, I made some quick calculations to assess the carbon offset by my solar powered phone charger on a larger scale. My two solar panelled phone charger uses 2 watts (W) of power (1W per solar panel) which offsets approximately 0.00219 tons, or 4.38 pounds of carbon annually, assuming Canada on average recieves 3kWh of sunlight. If my solar powered charger was mass produced up to 10000000 chargers that are regularly used, carbon reductions would grow linearly up to a reduction of 43800000lbs of carbon emissions annually, or 21900 tons. A graph and chart representing this data is shown below.

In comparison to Canadian carbon emissions, theoretically a solar powered phone charger like mine would offset 0.004% of Canada's carbon emissions if it was used on a regular basis by 10 million people (assuming each owns 1 solar phone charger). Although this may not seem like much, the carbon emissions from typical electric phone chargers on average account for 0.15% of Canada's total carbon emissions. Overall, today I assessed the positive environmental impacts of my charger, and I plan to represent the data in my report and presentation.
