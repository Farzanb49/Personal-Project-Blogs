Journal Entry #17: Assessing Product: Need for a diode?

Is the solution to our problems a diode? November 17, 2018

While testing my product, I recognized some discrepancies in my product regarding efficient and consistent charging. My phone would charge as it is supposed to, but with fluctuations. The charge sometimes went down before going back up. As a result of the fluctuations, the charging efficiency of my product is not as high as it should be. While thinking of and implicating solutions, such as switching the voltage regulator around, I recalled that many solar chargers, or electronic devices in general, used diodes to prevent the current from travelling in inconsistent directions, which is what could be affecting the charging capabilities of my portable phone charger. After determining this possible solution, I conducted some research on diodes and looked over other phone charger tutorials to determine the diode necessary for my product. However, I am unsure if adding a diode so late into the project is a wise idea, so to determine if it is a necessary component for my product, I will run a full test to see if my charger can fully charge a phone.
