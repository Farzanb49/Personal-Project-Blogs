Journal Entry #5:Remaining Components have Arrived!

Capacitors and solar panels came in the mail today!

Capacitors are a necessary electrical component because they make sure the current flows through the circuit without any ripples, thus keeping the current consistent, and preventing it from fluctuating. For this project, 2 types of capacitors are necessary. The first capacitor is a 2200uF (microfarad) capacitor, which is the bigger capacitor in this project, and the other is a 100uF capacitor. The reason behind why both a 2200uF capacitor and 100uF capacitor are needed involves some math, which I will look into with further detail in a later journal entry. The solar panels use solar energy to help power the product. The specifications for the solar panels are 6V and 1W per solar panel. I will use one solar panel for my first attempt at creating the phone charger. I may then attempt using 3 solar panels to provide more power output to the charger, which could help charge the phones faster. My goal with using solar energy is to create practical technology that can help offset carbon emissions using off grid energy sources. My next steps are probably drawing out schematics and planning the structure of my circuit, and to continue doing some research on the various components involved in this project. I will also provide some research related to renewable energy use, and analyse carbon emission offsets through use of renewable technology. I plan to complete most of my research before the school year starts. This will help me put more attention into constructing my device and give me more time to evaluate my product. I am planning on beginning construction of the project around the second week of September. I still need to obtain a soldering iron in order to construct my product. I also want to provide some more research before I begin constructing my product.
