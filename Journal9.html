Journal Entry #9: Learning about Breadboards

Learning about Breadboards! September 29, 2018

Today, I am finishing off my research on the components of my project. The three components I have little background knowledge of are voltage regulators, capacitors, and breadboards. I have gained some knowledge about voltage regulators and capacitors before and documented it in my process journal. Therefore, today, I learned about breadboards. A breadboard is a solderless device for temporary prototype with electronics and test circuit design. Basically, it is a great tool for experimenting with electrical components and creating prototype electrical devices. You can interconnect components in an electronic circuit by inserting their leads or terminals into the holes in the breadboard, and then making connections with them through wires where necessary. The breadboard has strips of metal underneath the board and connect the holes on the top of the board. Each row of holes on the sides of the breadboard are internally connected and each column of holes in the middle of the breadboard are internally connected, as illustrated in the diagram below. These interconnected holes are known as nodes. To interconnect two holes from different nodes, you have to use a cable connecting the two holes.
